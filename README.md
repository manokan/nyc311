## NYC 311 Service Requests (aka complaints)

Random parsings of the public dataset about most things that ail NYC. I started delving into 311 data several years ago after repeated complaints to 311 about late-night jackhammer drilling across the street from me kept getting closed out with a comment like: "on inspection our inspector was unable to find the condition reported." This was construction that lasted several years; a lot of sleepless nights. I may never have learned R but for those sleepless nights!

At some point, I realized that public servants are accountable to the public only indirectly, via our elected officials. In the case of NYC services, that is the Mayor and members of the city council. They run for office promising to improve quality-of-life, but their performance is not measurable, no metrics to point to. This gave me the idea to remap all data to city council districts.

Fast-forward to some months ago, when the council member from the Upper East Side tweeted, in response to a noise vizualization for his district, that perhaps the drop in noise in his district may be related to the evidence-based-governenace policies he/his office were pursuing. Possibly. That gave me the impetus to formalize what I was already doing into a 'governance-accountabiity' project. Make the mountains of public data useful by abstracting KPI buried within, tracing accountability, and testing performace hypotheses based on data rather than conjecture. 

Cleaning and parsing the roughly (at last count) 28 million rows is something I did incrementally over the months and years, and as needed for the [posts at DataTellsMe.Com](https://datatellsme.com). It's a bit of a disorganized mess (understatement alert). All of it will show up here as I clean up the code. For now, *in media res*, I will add the subsetted data in CSV form. Extensive comments help me remember, weeks later, what I was doing/trying to accomplish with a bit of code. Appreantly, a common problem! 

The folders are organized by visualization subject and contain the aggregated data needed to run the code. The full, raw datafiles are [here at the NYC Open Data Portal](https://data.cityofnewyork.us/browse?q=311).
